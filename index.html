Users include: 

        
 
            
Donor

            
Most blood donors are volunteers. Donors must meet certain criteria, such as being between 18 and 65 years old. 
            Donors must pass a physical exam and be free of certain health conditions. 
            Donors must be tested for infectious diseases like HIV, malaria, and hepatitis. 
            

            
Reciepients

            
Patients receive blood transfusions when they need a blood component, such as red blood cells, platelets, or plasma. 
            Blood banks test blood for safety before it's used in transfusions. 
            Blood banks match blood types so that the right blood is given to the right patient.
            

            
Blood Banks

            
Blood banks are usually located in hospitals, within a clinical pathology laboratory. 
            Blood banks perform pre-transfusion testing and blood compatibility testing. 
            Blood banks also store blood components until they are needed.
